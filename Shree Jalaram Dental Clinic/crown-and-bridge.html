Crown & Bridge

Crown

Sometimes referred to as caps, crowns cover a tooth entirely, restoring a natural shape and appearance. A crown may be needed for you to:

  • Cover a misshapen or discolored tooth
  • Protect a weak tooth
  • Restore a broken or worn tooth
  • Cover a tooth with a large filling
  • Hold a dental bridge in place
  • Cover a dental implant
  • Cover a tooth thatâ€™s had a root canal procedure

Glass, porcelain fused with metal, resin, or ceramic materials may be made from crowns. As crowns are expensive, they are usually recommended by dentists only when other procedures can not yield a pleasant result.



Bridge

Sometimes referred to as a fixed partial denture, to replace missing teeth with artificial teeth, bridges are used. Gold, alloys, porcelain, or a mixture can be made from bridges. After preparing them for crowns, dentists anchor them to the surrounding teeth. Then the crowns are connected by a false tooth, and the bridge is added to the prepared teeth. A fixed bridge can only be replaced by your dentist.

Your bridge's success depends upon its base. So, note that if you wear a bridge, oral hygiene is especially necessary in order to keep the remaining teeth safe.
